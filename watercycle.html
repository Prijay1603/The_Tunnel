The Water Cycle



The water cycle is a multi – step process of how the Earth’s water can be transported and what all stages it goes through. All of the stages in the water cycle are:

Evaporation.

Transpiration.

Condensation.

Precipitation.

Run – off.

Infiltration.

Each step is crucial to the water cycle. A brief summary is surface water is evaporated by the Sun’s heat. Water from plants is also evaporated. As the water vapor rises, it cools and condenses to form clouds. When the water droplets become too heavy, it precipitates. When the rain reaches the ground, some of it runs along the ground into collection places such as rivers and ponds. The rest of the water trickles into the ground to join an aquifer.
