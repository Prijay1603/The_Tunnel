Transpiration



Transpiration is one of steps in the water cycle. It is where the water from trees and plants is evaporated. Trees release water through pores called stone matta. These pores are located in the leaves and this water is considered a waste product. Plants are responsible for 10% of all water droplets in the atmosphere. On a hot day, each tree can lose around 400 litres of water depending on its size. This is not a bad thing. Because the water cycle is a closed cycle, the water does not simply disappear into thin air. Instead, this water creates clouds through condensation and so, it rains due to precipitation.
